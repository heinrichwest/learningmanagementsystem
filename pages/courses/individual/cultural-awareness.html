Cultural Awareness

Course Information

In today’s increasingly diverse South African workplace, understanding and appreciating cultural differences is vital to fostering inclusive, respectful, and productive environments. The Cultural Awareness course offers a foundational exploration of the diverse cultures present within the South African workforce. Through historical context, religious and political influence, and current workplace realities, learners will develop the knowledge and empathy required to promote harmony and equity across cultural boundaries. This course equips individuals to identify, address, and transform cultural challenges into opportunities for unity, cooperation, and shared success in a multicultural professional setting.

Level: All Levels Duration: 3h 10m Mode: Online

Unit 1: Analyse aspects of cultural diversity in the South African workplace

+
  • A listing is made of the cultures that make up the South African workplace.
  • A historical overview is provided of conflicts that have existed between various cultural groups in South Africa.
  • Examples are given of stereotyping and perceptions that have developed with respect to culture.

Unit 2: Discuss important generic aspects of cultural groups

+
  • A summary is given of the cultural activities and practices associated with South Africa’s mainstream religions.
  • The cultural views of at least three minor religions in South Africa are given.
  • The effect that politics has played in shaping cultural attitudes in South Africa is explained using historical and present-day examples.
  • The challenges inherent in creating a united
  • yet
  • culturally diverse workforce are explained with the use of examples of how the challenges may be overcome.

Unit 3: Understand the needs and sensitivities of people of different cultural backgrounds

+
  • The needs and sensitivities of people of different cultural backgrounds are identified with a view to understanding how best to meet their expectations.
  • The problems associated with the migrant labour system are explained in terms of the labourer’s personal development
  • feeling of self-worth and family ties and responsibilities.
  • Suggestions are offered for changes to policies
  • procedures
  • habits and attitudes in order to promote harmony in a culturally diverse workplace.
  • A distinction is made between fair and unfair discrimination pertaining to cultural difference as provided for in the South African constitution.
  • A range of “isms” and “phobias” relating to cultural difference is described in the context of the workplace.
